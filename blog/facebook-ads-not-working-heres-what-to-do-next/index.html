Facebook Ads Not Working? Here's What to Do Next

Stop Changing Everything at Once

When ads stop performing, the instinct is to panic and start changing things. New creative, new audiences, new campaign structure, new offer. All at once. And then when something starts working again, you have no idea which change fixed it. Or worse, three of your changes made things better and two made them worse, and the net effect is nothing.

The first rule when ads stop working: slow down and diagnose before you treat. You wouldn't take five different medications at once to fix a headache. Same principle applies here.

Follow the Numbers to the Problem

Every ad campaign is a chain. Impression to click. Click to landing page. Landing page to conversion. Conversion to sale. The break is somewhere in that chain, and the data tells you where.

Start from the top and work down:

  • CPM (cost per thousand impressions) — if this spiked, it's an auction or audience issue. Your ads might have creative fatigue, or you're competing against a seasonal rush of advertisers.
  • CTR (click-through rate) — if this dropped, your creative isn't resonating. The hook is dead, the visual is stale, or the audience has seen it too many times.
  • Landing page conversion rate — if clicks are coming but nobody's converting, the page is the problem. Message mismatch, slow load speed, or a weak offer.
  • Cost per acquisition — if leads are coming but they're not buying, the issue is downstream. Sales process, lead quality, or offer-market fit.

Each of these breakpoints has a different fix. Treating a landing page problem with new ad creative is like changing your tires when your engine is broken.

The Creative Fatigue Problem

The most common reason ads "stop working" is simple creative fatigue. Your audience has seen the same ad too many times. The frequency climbs, the CTR drops, and the algorithm starts struggling to find people who haven't already been exposed.

The fix isn't one new ad. It's a system for continuously producing new creative. You should be launching new ad variations every 1-2 weeks at minimum. Not wholesale changes to your messaging — variations on proven themes. Different hooks, different visuals, different formats of the same core message.

When It's Not an Ad Problem at All

Sometimes your ads are fine and the problem is somewhere else entirely. Your landing page broke on mobile. Your calendar booking link is glitchy. Your sales team changed their approach. Your competitor launched a better offer.

Before you touch a single thing in Ads Manager, check everything downstream. Open your landing page on your phone. Test the form. Check your email deliverability. Listen to recent sales calls. The number of times a "Facebook ads problem" turned out to be a broken form or a deliverability issue is embarrassingly high.
