How to Sign $100k+ Clients From Paid Ads (info, Services, and Funds)

High-Ticket Clients Don't Buy the Way You Think

There's a misconception that signing $100K+ clients requires fancy proposals, months of relationship building, and a sales process that looks like enterprise software. Sometimes it does. But more often, the bottleneck isn't your sales process — it's that you're not getting in front of enough of the right people.

Paid ads solve the top-of-funnel problem. They put your message in front of thousands of potential high-ticket buyers every day. But the way you run those ads, and what happens after someone clicks, has to match the stakes of the deal.

The Funnel Structure for Six-Figure Deals

When someone is considering spending $100K or more, they need to believe three things before they'll take a call. First, that you deeply understand their specific situation. Second, that you've solved this problem for people like them before. Third, that the risk of working with you is lower than the risk of doing nothing.

Your funnel needs to build all three beliefs before the sales conversation even starts. That usually means a longer-form piece of content — a webinar, a case study walkthrough, or a detailed strategy presentation — that does the heavy lifting. The ad drives traffic to this content. The content does the selling. The call is just the close.

This is the opposite of the typical "book a call" ad that most people run. Those work for $3K-$10K offers. At $100K+, you need to earn the right to even ask for someone's time.

Different Models, Same Principles

Whether you're selling a high-ticket info product, a done-for-you service, or raising capital for a fund, the core principles don't change much:

  • Lead with insight, not features — show you understand their world better than they expect
  • Use proof that matches their context — a SaaS founder doesn't care about your e-commerce results
  • Qualify aggressively before the call — protect your calendar from people who can't afford you
  • Let the content handle the education so the call can focus on fit and terms

The info product version might use a VSL. The service version might use a case study presentation. The fund version might use a market analysis webinar. But the underlying architecture is the same — create belief, qualify, then convert.

The Real Bottleneck at This Level

Most people who struggle to sign high-ticket clients don't have an ad problem. They have a positioning problem. Their messaging sounds like everyone else in their space. Their proof is generic. Their qualification is too loose. When you fix those three things, the ads almost run themselves. A well-positioned offer to a well-qualified audience with strong proof will close at rates that make the math easy. A poorly positioned offer with weak proof will burn through ad spend no matter how good your targeting or creative is. Fix the offer first, then scale the ads.
