How to Launch High Ticket Info Into Paid Ads After Spending $600k in 2025

$600K in 2025 Bought Expensive Lessons

Spending $600K on ads in a year teaches you things that no course, mastermind, or YouTube video can. Some of those lessons came from winning. Most of them came from losing. And the difference between the two usually came down to timing, creative quality, and how fast we adapted when something stopped working.

If you're sitting on a high-ticket info product and you're thinking about turning on paid ads for the first time, the playbook in 2026 looks different than it did even 12 months ago. The platform has changed, buyer behavior has shifted, and the competition for attention has increased.

What Changed from 2025 to 2026

The biggest shift is in creative velocity. In 2025, you could find a winning ad and ride it for 4-6 weeks. In 2026, the half-life of a winning creative has shrunk to 2-3 weeks. Audiences are seeing more ads, scrolling faster, and developing banner blindness quicker.

That means the game isn't about finding one perfect ad. It's about building a system that produces new variations faster than the old ones fatigue. The businesses scaling successfully right now are the ones with creative machines — structured processes for ideating, producing, and testing new ads every week.

The other big change is in audience behavior. Cold traffic is colder than it used to be. People need more touches before they trust enough to spend $5K+ on a high-ticket product. That means longer nurture sequences, more retargeting layers, and content that builds familiarity over days and weeks, not a single landing page.

The Launch Strategy for High-Ticket

Launching a high-ticket product into paid ads isn't the same as launching a $97 course. The economics are different, the sales cycle is different, and the funnel architecture is different.

  • Start with a presentation-style funnel — a webinar, workshop, or case study walkthrough that gives value and pre-sells the call
  • Budget $10-20K for the initial testing phase — plan for 60-90 days before expecting ROI
  • Build your creative library before you launch — have 10-15 ad variations ready on day one
  • Set up a sales team, not just a booking page — high-ticket cold traffic needs a real closer, not a self-serve checkout

The Lesson Worth $600K

The single most expensive lesson from 2025: don't scale before you've found product-market fit for your ads. Not product-market fit for your offer — you might have an offer people love. But advertising product-market fit is different. It means you've found the creative angle, audience, and funnel combination that produces customers profitably. Scaling before you have that is pouring fuel on a fire that isn't lit yet. Spend the time and money in the testing phase to find the combination that works. Once you have it, scaling is just turning a dial. Before you have it, scaling is just burning money faster.
