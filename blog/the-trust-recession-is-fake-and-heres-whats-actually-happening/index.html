the "trust recession" is fake and here's what's actually happening

Why is the trust recession narrative really just a sales pitch?

I hear it all the time from prospects, other marketers, and operators in the info space. "We're in a trust recession. Leads are so hard to sell." It's a bunch of smoke and mirrors. The trust recession is a false belief that doesn't serve you unless you're using it to sell something. It's just market sophistication. The market has gotten smarter, and the lazy tactics that worked in 2016 don't work anymore. That's not a recession. That's called evolution.

All the proprietary systems and unique mechanisms people are pitching right now? It's the 2025 version of the Lamborghini in the driveway. It's just another hook to get you to buy. "The trust recession is so bad, you need MY special system to fix it." No you don't. Direct response ads work. Call funnels work. Low ticket funnels work. Webinars work. They all still work.

What has actually changed in the market if not trust?

The difference is you can't just sell the opportunity anymore. You need to disqualify other opportunities. You need to come across as a professional. You need to address things in enough depth that people can tell you actually know your craft. That's not a trust recession - that's a higher bar for competence, and it should be. The shiny object stuff is just a distraction designed to redirect your attention toward someone else's solution.

Instead of buying into the narrative, look at three things when evaluating anyone in this space. What level of conviction do they speak with? Are they competent in the technical details? And do they talk to you like an adult? Especially in the high ticket space, if you're selling to people with real money, be direct. Do you want to solve this problem? Here's how we help. Forty seconds. Book a call. It can genuinely be that simple.

Do all funnel types still work in the current market?

Any marketer who tells you a specific funnel type doesn't work anymore is really just telling you they're not good at it. That's fine - everyone has strengths. But be aware of the framing. Call funnels work for people who are good at call funnels. Low ticket ascension works for people who've dialed that in. The underlying principles haven't changed. What sales need to happen to move a prospect from A to B? Answer that question and you can make any funnel model work.

Where is the real opportunity if the trust recession is fake?

This is where the lazy marketers and sales reps get weeded out and the best ones rise to the top. If you can speak with conviction, demonstrate competence, and treat your prospects like intelligent adults, you'll crush it right now. I've spent over seven figures on paid ads and I can tell you that what works hasn't fundamentally changed. The people who are struggling are the ones who relied on hype and shortcuts. If that's not you, there's never been a better time to be in this space.

Stop buying into the trust recession narrative and start being direct. The market isn't broken. The bar just went up. And if you can clear it - by being specific, being competent, and being straightforward - you'll find less competition at the top than you expected, because most people quit when the easy tactics stopped working.
