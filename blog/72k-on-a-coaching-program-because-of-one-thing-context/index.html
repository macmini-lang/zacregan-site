$72k on a Coaching Program Because of One Thing (context)

Information Isn't the Bottleneck

Everything you need to know to build a successful business is free on the internet. Every strategy, every tactic, every framework. It's all out there. So why do people still pay $10K, $25K, $72K for coaching programs? Because information isn't the bottleneck. Context is.

Context is knowing which piece of information to apply to your specific situation, right now, at this stage, with these resources. That's the thing you can't get from a YouTube video or a course. A YouTube video tells you 15 things that might work. Context tells you the one thing that will work for you this quarter.

What $72K in Context Looks Like

When I invested $72K in a coaching program, I didn't learn anything I hadn't already heard before. The strategies weren't secret. The frameworks weren't novel. What I got was someone looking at my specific business, my specific numbers, and my specific situation and telling me exactly where to focus.

That's worth more than a library of courses because it eliminates the most expensive cost in business — time spent on the wrong things. Every week you spend working on the wrong priority is a week you could have spent on the right one. At $100K/month in revenue, a wasted month costs you $100K plus the opportunity cost of what you didn't do.

The coaching didn't give me new information. It gave me the ability to ignore 90% of the information I already had and focus on the 10% that mattered for my situation. That filter is what context provides.

When the Investment Makes Sense

Not every coaching investment is worth it. The value of context scales with your current level. If you're at $0 in revenue, context is less valuable because you haven't hit the problems that context solves. You need information first — the basics of how things work. But once you've built something, once you're generating revenue, context becomes the accelerator.

  • Below $10K/month — you probably need information (courses, free content, trial and error)
  • $10K-$50K/month — context starts becoming valuable, a $5-15K investment can compress 6 months into 6 weeks
  • $50K-$200K/month — context is the highest-leverage investment you can make, the right advice at this stage is worth multiples of what you pay
  • $200K+ — you're paying for access to people operating at the level you're trying to reach, proximity matters as much as advice

How to Evaluate Whether You Need It

If you know what to do but you're not sure which thing to do first, you need context. If you have three possible strategies and you're paralyzed choosing between them, you need context. If your business is growing but you feel like you're working on things that don't move the needle, you need context. The question isn't whether coaching programs are "worth it" in the abstract. The question is whether you're at a stage where having someone filter your options and point you toward the right priority would save you more time and money than the program costs. At $72K, the answer was clearly yes. One right decision at that stage paid for the program in the first month.
