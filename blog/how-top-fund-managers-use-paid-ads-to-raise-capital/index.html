How Top Fund Managers Use Paid Ads to Raise Capital

Fund Managers Are Waking Up to Paid Ads

For decades, capital raising meant networking events, country club dinners, and warm introductions through your existing LP base. That still works. But the fund managers who are scaling fastest right now have added a channel that most of their competitors still dismiss — paid advertising.

The math is simple. A placement agent takes 1-3% of capital raised. A paid ads operation generating qualified investor leads costs a fraction of that per dollar raised. And unlike a placement agent, paid ads don't stop working when the relationship goes cold.

What the Ad Strategy Actually Looks Like

Fund managers aren't running the same playbook as info product companies. The audience is different, the compliance environment is different, and the sales cycle is longer. But the core principles still apply — you're putting a message in front of a specific person and asking them to take a specific action.

The best-performing strategies we've seen combine a few elements:

  • Educational content ads that position the GP as a thought leader in their asset class
  • Webinar or presentation funnels that provide genuine market analysis
  • Application-style qualification that pre-screens for accredited investor status
  • Retargeting sequences that build familiarity over weeks, not days

The key difference from typical direct response is patience. You're not trying to close someone on the first touch. You're trying to start a relationship with someone who has $250K+ to deploy and needs to trust you before they wire it.

The Content That Actually Works

Fund managers who try to run ads that look like fund managers wrote them usually fail. "Seeking accredited investors for our 506(c) offering with a 14% IRR target" might be accurate, but it reads like a compliance document, not an ad.

The content that performs is educational and slightly contrarian. A GP who runs a multifamily fund doesn't lead with "invest in apartments." They lead with "here's what's actually happening in multifamily right now that most investors are missing." That's the hook. The fund is the solution they discover after they've already been educated.

Video performs particularly well in this space because trust matters more than almost any other vertical. LPs need to see the GP speak, hear their conviction, and assess their competence before they'll take a meeting. A 3-minute video ad can do more for trust-building than a 20-page pitch deck.

Why Most Fund Managers Get It Wrong

The most common mistake is treating paid ads like a transaction. Run ads, get leads, close them. That works for a $997 course. It doesn't work when someone is wiring you half a million dollars.

The fund managers who succeed with paid ads treat it as the top of a relationship funnel. The ad starts the conversation. The content builds credibility. The application qualifies. The call builds rapport. The follow-up nurtures. And eventually, months later sometimes, the capital comes in. The ones who expect immediate returns from a $5K ad spend get frustrated and quit. The ones who understand they're building a pipeline that compounds over time end up raising more capital than they ever did through traditional channels.
